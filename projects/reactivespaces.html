Wings of Freedom

The purpose of the interactive installation is to raise awareness about the on-going refugee crisis. Gentle wing flapping movement of the birds passively invites the passersby. The plight of refugees as they struggle to move towards a better future is depicted in this slow flapping motion of birds as if they are struggling to take flight. Viewers can tweet their message of encouragement for the refugees and include the provided tag #flighttofreedom, which changes the flapping movement of the birds in a way that they appear to be in a more faster and harmonious motion.

Battle of the Screams

Carnegie Library Knoxville serves one of Pittsburgh’s highest youth population. It offers resources and run programs for children and teens in the neighbourhood. Not only it gives them access to reading materials but also to computers. It is in a unique position where it could help the neighbourhood's children in technological literacy. The concept was to create a fun game for the children at the library, which at the same time creates a curiosity about underlying technology in them.
The game is played between 2 children standing on either side of the wall and screaming in the mic. Based on the intensity of their sound there will be sweeping motions(leveraging the lights in the installation)from both ends of the wall, and sweeps will collide with each other. If both children are making sound at the same intensity the sweep motion will start from their respective end and stop in the middle. But, if someone’s intensity is greater than the other’s one, their sweep will overpower the other’s one and that person will win.
