Hi|Estòria

Share your story

Overview

Hi|Estória is a mobile application that allows community members to share stories of personal and public history and place those stories in specific locations in their community. The system allows both visitors and community members to experience the history of a place by combining crowd-sourced stories with location-based interaction, in turn, creating a community walking museum.

Team & Duration

Role

User Experience Researcher & Product Designer

Tools

Methods

participatory design, personas and scenarios, competitive analysis, qualitative validation, system design

Brief

The Beanstalk Network is a research project of the Madeira Interactive Technologies Institute whose goal is to have a better understanding of both locals and tourists movements around the island of Madeira to analyze trends in tourism and marketing. Our challenge was to design a project that uses the existing infrastructure of wifi routers on buses around the island to create a design concept that serves the needs of local communities.

NeedFinding

Using participatory design to learn ideas and needs of the community

We started by conducting a participatory design session with local high school students. While we needed to design something based on the Beanstalk Network's capabilities, we didn't want to constrain our participants' ideas. So, we kept the focus of the session about their experiences on buses and their hopes and ideas for their community.

Findings

  • Unsurprisingly, the students use their smartphones a lot. Most of our participants sketched smartphones and headphones as the items they always bring on the bus.
  • Our participants wanted to learn more about the history of their city.
  • They want to spend more time engaging with others in the community.
  • They understood the value of tourism in Machico's economy.

Setting Hunt Statement

Based on the findings from the participatory session, we decided to design a walking museum of Machico history with content and stories that are created and curated by locals that uses the Beanstalk bus wifi system as an entry point and enabling technology. But we still had a lot of questions about system discovery, moderation, incentives, and content.

Competitive Analysis

Learning from existing solutions

We researched for similar apps, and did competitive analysis for popular apps like Periscope and services such as Murmur, 7Scenes and Humans of New York. We tested each of them to identify their strengths and weaknesses.

Findings

  • Content from real people is far more engaging than scripted content.
  • Confusing interfaces are quickly abandoned.
  • Cues in physical environment seemed to increase engagement.

Personas and Scenarios

We created 2 personas to envision our system - Mariana, a local high school student interested in history and journalism; Manuel, a man who grew up in Machico and knows a lot about the history of Machico;
We then moved on to enivision context scenarios to explore how Mariana and Manuel will achieve their goals with the system. We invision the system so that anyone can add content, but we also wanted a group from the community to guide the system. We pictured that a local high school club could add important stories by interviewing people like Manuel.

Design Decisions

Moderation by linking facebook profile

Using a social network login with emphasis accountability and sense of community among users.

Restricting content to audio

Stories are recoded as audio by the users and they can tag it to relevant locations.

Context-Aware Suggestions

The app suggests you near by stories, and they can also opt in to notifications, which would let them know when they are passing near a story.

Allowing Exploration

The app allows users to search for specific locations if they want to explore and see if there is any stories associated to location of their interest.


System Validation

We went back to the original partipicants and new community members with our storyboards and medium fidelity prototypes to get their feedback about the system. We received positive feedback throughout and all of them shared different ideas about the kind of stories they would like to see in the service.

"If the system exists, I would just go out for a walk just to hear new stories"

"I absolutely loved the concept of a walking Machico Musuem"

Reflection

This was my first opportunity to conduct a participatory session and engage with a local community to include them in the design process. We learned so much about their needs, attitudes, motivations and I realised people can be really creative if you can provide them a good framework. Also, the fact that we were able to go back to them to get their feedback on our design solution not only gave us future improvements, but also a sense of closure.
