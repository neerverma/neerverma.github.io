Quinta Annual Festival

Celebrate with your Community

Overview

A service Experience for the annual festival of a local Quinta(a country estate) in Madeira, Portugal where the members of the local community gathers to get involve in traditional cultural events and workshops.

Team

Mike Henderson, Carolina Gouveia

Role

Service Designer

Tools

Sketch, Framer, Premier

Methods

Ethnographic interviews, Competitive analysis, Service blueprinting, Wireframing, Prototyping

Brief

Quinta Da sa Luzia is an old Quinta(a country estate) at Maderia, which organises an annual festival for the local community with local traditional events. Here, our challenge was to create a service experience for the festival.

Final Service Design Experience



Understanding the users

Interviewing different stakeholders

We interviewed various stakeholders including the owner of the Quinta, event performers, workshop organizers and attendees. From the interviews we created a service ecology map.

Service Safaris to understand the customers journeys

We were lucky enough to attend this years festival and being able to talk to some of the attendees. This enabled us to create a customer journey map for the attendees.

Service Proposal and Service Blueprints

We conducted a brainstorming session on "how might we" solve for issues identified at various stages of customer journey. Our new proposed service tackles the issues identified in the research, builds on the current relationship between the stakeholders by leveraging upon existing systems. Our proposed service has the following 3 main components.

  • A Resposive Website
  • A Co-Creation Chatbot
  • Physical artifacts at the event for navigation

We felt that it would be easier to communicate how the service works and its value if we build 2 service blueprints from the perpective of both the customers; the event attendees and the event hosts.

The service experience could be broken down in two phases: Preparation and Attending the festival.

We also realised that there are main phases that each of our stakeholders goes through. First, the preparation for the event and second, attending the event.
Here are the final service blueprints:

Responsive Website

A Responsive website for attendees.

Submit Event Proposal

The responsive website allows the event hosts to submit their event proposals to be reviewed by Quinta Organising committee.

Show interest in Events

Event attendees can show interest to events. Afterwards, they are invited for a group chat with the host facilitated by the chatbot.

Festival Schedule

The responsive website has the festival schedule and also provides more information about all events.

Saving your favorite events

Users can save the certain events as favorite and they get notified when those event are about to start.

Navigating within festival

The app has a local map of the quinta premises and allows users to navigate during the festival where they can see which event is being held at a particular location at any specific time.

Co-creation Chatbot

A facebook chatbot facilitating co-creation of the festival.

Introducing hosts and attendees

The chatbot initiates the conversation between hosts and attendees by prompting the host and the attendees to introduce themselves.

Balancing goals of host and attendees

The chatbot moves the conversation forward by prompting both the host and attendees to have a discussion about their goals for the events and moderating the conversation.

Facilitating logistics and scheduling

The chatbot creates a poll for attendees to choose the time that suits all of them and subsequently, sets up calendar events and updates the website with the date and time.

Encouraging participation

The chatbot keeps track of whether the conversation is moving forward or not. If there is no messages exchanged for a while, it identifies the person who might or should respond and prompts them to reply.

REFLECTIONS

It was a totally unique experience as most of the stakeholders were involved with the festival on a personal level and we really needed to dig deep to understand their motivations and goals. Moreover, I learnt that it is important to make sure that local knowledge is considered and integrated in the service experience. I appreciate being able to attend the festival and get feedback about our proposed designs.
