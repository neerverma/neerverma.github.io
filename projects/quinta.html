Quinta Annual Festival

Celebrate with your Community

Overview

A service Experience for the annual festival of a local Quinta(a country estate) at Funchal, Portugal where the members of the local community gathers to get involve in traditional cultural events and workshops.

Team & Duration

Mike Henderson, Carolina Gouveia | 5 Weeks

Role

Service Designer

Tools

Sketch, Framer, Premier

Methods

Ethnographic interviews, personas and scenarios, competitive analysis, service blueprint, customer journey map, wireframing, prototyping and video making

Brief

Quinta Da sa Luzia is an old Quinta(a country estate) at Funchal Maderia, which organises an annual festival for the local community involving local traditional events. Here, our challenge was to create a service experience for all stakeholders in the festival.

Final Service Design Experience



Understanding the users

Interviewing different stakeholders of the festival

We interviewed all the stakeholders- owner of the Quinta, event performers, workshop organizers and attendees. From the interviews we created a service ecology map.

Service Safaris to understand the customers journeys

We were lucky enough to attend this years festival and to talk to some of the attendees. This enabled us to create a customer journey map for the attendees.

Service Proposal and Service Blueprints

We conducted a brainstorming session on "how might we" solve for issues identified at various stages of customer journey. Our new proposed service tackles the issues identified in the research, builds on the current relationship between the stakeholders by leveraging upon existing systems and introducing few new components. Our proposed service has the following 3 main components.

  • A Resposive Website
  • A Co-Creation Chatbot
  • Physical artifacts at the event for navigation

We felt that it would be easier to communicate how the service works and its value if we build 2 service blueprints from the perpective of both the customers; the event attendees and the event hosts.

The service experience could be broken down in two phases - Preparation for festival and Attending the festival.

We also realised that there are two main phases that each of our stakeholders goes through, first, the preparation for the event and second, attending the event. Here are the final service blueprints:

Responsive Website

A Responsive website for attendees.

Submit Event Proposal

The responsive website will allow the event hosts to submit their event proposals to be reviewed by Quinta Organising committee.

Show interest in Events

Event attendees can show interest to events, and afterwards, they are invited in a group chat with the host facilitated by the chatbot.

Festival Schedule

The responsive website has the festival schedule, where users can also see more information about any event.

Save your favorite events

Users can save the certain events as favorite and they get notified when the event is about to start.

Navigating within festival

The app has a local map of the quinta premises and allows users to navigate during the festival where they can see which event is being held at a particular location at any specific time.

Co-creation Chatbot

A facebook chatbot facilitating co-creation of festival events.

Introducing hosts and attendees

The chatbot initiates the conversation between hosts and attendees by prompting first the host and then the attendees to introduce themselves.

Balancing goals of host and attendees

The chatbot moves the conversation forward by prompting both the host and attendees to have a discussion about their goals for the events and moderating the conversation.

Facilitating logistics and scheduling

The chatbot creates a poll for attendees to choose between time convenient for them and sets up calendar events for each of them, as well as updates the website with the finalized date and time.

Encouraging participation

The chatbot keeps track of whether the conversation is moving forward or not, and if there is no messages exchanged for some time, it identifies the person who should respond and tags that person to reply.

REFLECTIONS

This was really a unique experience as most of the stakeholders involved weren't part of the festival for any monetory gain, but just for the chance to interact with their community. This forced us to dig deep to understand their personal motivations and goals, and opprotunity areas to build a service experience which is in line with their motivations and goals. Additionally, this allowed me to experience a foreign local culture and taught me the importantance of building empathy while designing.
