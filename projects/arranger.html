Arranger

Get your ride

Overview

Arranger is an App which allows tween to setup their own playdates and events by requesting parents for approval and rides.

Team & Duration

Daya Lee | 3 Weeks

Role

Product Designer

Tools

Sketch, Framer

Methods

Interviews, Personas and Scenarios, System design, Wireframing, Speed Dating

Brief

Tweens these days are engaged in various social and interpersonal development activities such as theatre, football, music or hanging out at cafes with friends. We were given the problem of improving communication between tweens and parents within the realm of event planning.

Final Design Overview

Ecosystem dump

We started with ecosystem dump, and determined that the areas we most needed to focus our research on were how tweens planned small social events, how comfortable parents are with others driving their kids places (other parents, public transportation, uber), and where kids often hang out. We also decided to get an idea of how tweens in a couple of different age groups and independence levels managed their independence with their parentsâ€™ expectations.

NeedFinding

Conducting one on one interviews

We interviewed 5 tweens and 6 parents, to understand how tween events are co-ordinated and what are the associated pain-points. Some parents also shared artifacts like calendar or bulletin boards that they use to co-ordinate activities of their kids.

Findings

  • Parents usually grant permission to tween activities, but this doesn't translate to transporting them around.
  • They don't like it when tweens assume they are free.
  • Parents want tweens to be independent in making their plans.
  • Parents are worried about their children safety when they are out.

Personas and Storyboards

Building on our insights from research, we developed the two primary personas - Tween persona and Parent persona.

Using Context scenarios with the Personas to come up with the interaction framework of our app

How might we enable tweens to be more responsible

How might we enable better scheduling

How might we reduce the safety concerns of parents


System Design

We decided on a system where -

  • Tweens will request the parent to go to any event or ride by providing them complete information about the event.
  • Parents approve tweens requests.
  • Parents can see real time location of the tween.

We decided that we will have 2 app versions, one for Parents and one for Tweens.

Iterations

Starting of with basic Wireframes

Keeping in mind the features that both parent and tween apps would have to facilitate, we quickly laid out basic wireframes to outline the navigation of the app using key path scenarios and iteratively refining them.

User Testing with Medium fidelity Wireframes

The screens were quickly mocked up in Sketch for peer feedback to get insights on whether the screens were able to acheive the goals of the system.

Design Decisions

Replacing request form to a conversational UI

Based on the feedback during critique and user testing sessions that a filling a request form will be really tedious, we decided to move away from a request form to a conversational UI for requesting parents.

Allowing tweens to text their friends from within the application

Another thing that came up was the need for the scenario when parents approve you to go the event, but couldn't give you a ride. We decided that the app would allow them to reach out to their friends, who in turn could use the app to request a ride from their parents.
