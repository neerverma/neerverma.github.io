Trickle

Data Leak

Overview

Public Wi-Fi networks at airports, restaurants and cafes is an amazing perk, but it comes at a risk. It is very easy for anyone to access data about your online activity if you are connected to a public Wi-Fi. We wanted to inform the public about this and chose to tell this story through a light and sound installation.

Team & Duration

Gillian Johnson, Arnita Saini | 4 Weeks

Role

Tools

Methods

Prototyping and Video making

Installation Video

 

Concept

The goal of the installation is to raise awareness in people about the issue of data theft. When someone connects to our fake public Wi-Fi network, their online activity is tracked and our installation shows a trickling down pattern using lights.

The installation gets activated as it identifies when a person who has been compromised passes near the installation. The installation uses different colors of lights that relate to different apps and services that are identified in person's online activity. Also, when an incoming notification on any of those apps is received the notification sound of that app is played by the installation - to re-inforce threat to personal data.

User Flow

Making

One of the biggest challanges we as a group faced was deciding on the technology we were going to use and figuring out how to use it. As our project had two major components (setting up a twin wifi network for people to connect to so that we could track their data and creating a responsive light installation that senses open wi-fi networks passing by).Eventually we were able to bring it all together and make adjustments when necessary, as our ideas evolved throughout the creation of this project.

Site Installation

We wanted to position our installation in a public Wi-Fi environment and we chose a nearby Starbucks. Prior to actually filming the video, we did video storyboard for the whole narrative. The, we recorded our script and although there were some logistic challenges, we eventually overcame all issues and shot our video . It was pretty wonderful to see the lighting interaction actually work and the user’s movements were being tracked.
